Degree of Experiential Learning

5
Extensive & Required
  • Required internship or capstone
  • Direct work with external partner, real deliverable
  • Strong industry connections (pro teams, business, health)
  • Strong faculty support and sports-world leadership
4
Required with Real-World Application
  • Required internship or capstone
  • Work with real-world data and application
  • Strong external partners, non-recurring
3
Present but Limited
  • Non-required or loosely embedded internship/capstone
  • Class projects, simulations, or case studies
  • Only one weak external partner
2
Surface-Level Exposure
  • No required internship or capstone
  • Sports examples in class, no real application
  • Credits toward degree without sport focus
1
No Experiential Component
  • No internship, capstone, or external partners
  • In-class or online learning only
  • Student club at most

Degree of Technical Learning

5
Highly Technical
  • Strong foundation: Python, SQL, R, data viz, AI modeling
  • Heavy statistical coursework
  • Analytics-driven curriculum throughout
4
Technical-Leaning
  • Emphasizes technical skill over stakeholder work
  • Focused on gathering and processing data
  • Applied context present but secondary
3
Balanced
  • Multiple technical and quantitative courses
  • Meaningful sports-specific application
  • Emphasis on internship or external client work
2
Applied-Leaning
  • Uses data but technical depth is limited
  • Analytics support decisions, don't drive curriculum
  • Research projects with real clients
1
Strongly Applied
  • Heavily focused on applied contexts and communication
  • Little to no technical rigor
  • Consistent work with external partners
